### Dimension D3 — Structural necessity

> **Question**: Can WordPress architecture function without
> Mediation-like structures, or are they constitutive?

**Counterfactual analysis**:
- Without Mediation: WordPress security model collapses
  (no capability gates, no nonce verification, no permission
  callbacks)
- Without Mediation: User experience model collapses
  (no UX-guidance gates like inserter availability,
  block constraints)
- Without Mediation: Authority access architecture collapses
  (no controlled access to authority resources)

**Verdict**: Mediation-like structures are STRUCTURALLY
NECESSARY for WordPress. They are not ornamental; they are
constitutive.

But this raises a refined question:
> Is structural necessity sufficient for KB-Wide LAW status?

- Many implementation patterns are structurally necessary
  (e.g., specific PHP functions, specific hook patterns) but
  NOT KB-Wide LAW-worthy
- Necessity ≠ architectural invariance
- KB-Wide LAW requires INVARIANT STRUCTURE, not just

> ARCHITECTURAL INVARIANCE.

LAW-WORTHY architectural invariant (if any) is the
**Declaration ≠ Exposure** principle (Law 1) which Mediation
implements. The implementations are necessary BECAUSE Law 1
is invariant; Mediation is NOT separately invariant.

**Phase 8.6 verdict on D3**: Mediation is structurally
NECESSARY but the necessity flows from Law 1 (Declaration ≠
Exposure) which Mediation implements. Necessity does not
warrant KB-Wide LAW promotion — Law 1 already captures the
invariance; Mediation captures the implementation.
