## SECTION C — Constitutional Gate Evolution

KB now operates **3 audit gate classes** as **jurisprudential
infrastructure**. Gate evolution mirrors KB's constitutional
maturation arc.

> **KB no longer just governs patterns. KB governs HOW
> PATTERNS ARE CONSTITUTIONALLY ADMITTED.**

### 3 Audit Gate Classes (Phase 8.14)

#### Gate Class 1 — Standard Gate (Phase 7.8 origin)

**5 criteria**:
1. Bounded context PRESENCE ≥ 4
2. Architectural variants ≥ 2
3. Intra-context density ≥ 1
4. Q10 sub-pattern check completed
5. Forward + retroactive evidence both contributing

**Applies to**: Operational pattern candidates (selection /
actualization / composition)

**Historical applications**:
- Phase 7.8 Resolution audit (5/5 met → REFUSED on
  structural fit; first refusal)
- Phase 8.13 Bridge audit (5/5 met → REFUSED + LAW
  SUB-PATTERN FORMALIZED)

#### Gate Class 2 — Governance-Intensive Gate (Phase 8.5 origin)

**7 criteria** (5 Standard + 2 governance-specific):
1-5. Standard criteria
6. Gating abstraction independence
7. Structural consequence

**Applies to**: Governance-mechanism candidates (gating /
mediation / access control)

**Historical applications**:
- Phase 8.x Mediation initial audit (6/7 fully MET + 1
  PARTIALLY → Doctrine-tier PROMOTED)
- Phase 8.6 Mediation re-audit (7/7 fully MET → KB-Wide
  RE-REFUSED + Doctrine RETAINED)

#### Gate Class 3 — Law Sub-pattern Gate (Phase 8.14 origin; NEW v2)

**4 criteria** (formalization criteria; applied IN ADDITION
TO Standard or Governance-intensive admissibility gate):
1. **L1 — 100% parent-law dependence** (ALL instances
   manifest parent law)
2. **L2 — Strong cross-context breadth** (≥3-4 character
   categories)
3. **L3 — Structural specificity beyond parent law's general
   invariant** (direction / medium / boundary type
   specificity)
4. **L4 — Sub-character coherence** (multiple instances
   share specialization invariant)

**Applies to**: Law sub-pattern formalization candidates
(when Q11 outcome (e) reached)

**Historical applications**:
- Phase 8.13 Bridge audit: Law Sub-pattern Gate 4/4 MET →
  Law 3b formalized (1st Law sub-pattern formalization in
  KB)

#### Gate Class hierarchy

| dimension | Standard | Governance-Intensive | Law Sub-pattern |
|---|---|---|---|
| Number of criteria | 5 | 7 | 4 |
| Function | admissibility | admissibility | formalization |
| Application timing | initial audit | initial audit | additional gate when Q11 outcome (e) |
| Strictness | baseline | strengthened | **strictest** (alters law-tier architecture) |
| Constitutional commitment | KB-Wide LAW promotion possible | KB-Wide LAW or Doctrine promotion possible | LAW SUB-PATTERN promotion (alters hierarchy) |

### Gate evolution narrative

> **KB's audit gate infrastructure evolved from singular to
> bifurcated to TRIFURCATED.** Each gate addition addresses
> distinct constitutional question:
>
> - **Standard**: "Does this candidate have sufficient

Each gate class is jurisprudential infrastructure — KB

## SECTION D — Q11 Evolution (Constitutional Adjudication Engine)

Q11 has evolved through 3 stages, becoming KB's
**constitutional adjudication engine**. Q11's progression
demonstrates KB's legal maturation.

### Q11 stages

#### Q11 v1 — 3-outcome model (Phase 8.5 origin)

> "Does audit evidence indicate this candidate is better
> constitutionalized as Doctrine-tier rather than KB-Wide
> LAW tier?"

**3 outcomes**:
- (a) Promote (KB-Wide LAW tier elevation)
- (b) Refuse (tier denied entirely)
- (c) Doctrine-tier formal promotion

**Constitutional purpose**: Distinguished promote / doctrine /
refuse outcomes. Replaced binary "promote-or-refuse" framing.

#### Q11 v2 — 4-outcome model (Phase 8.7 codification)

Phase 8.6 Mediation re-audit produced "Refine" precedent —
re-audit may PRESERVE TIER while REFINING doctrine
ARCHITECTURE.

**4 outcomes**:
- (a) Promote
- (b) Refuse
- (c) Retain (tier maintained at current level)
- **(d) Refine (NEW Phase 8.7)** — re-audit refinement;
  tier preserved + architecture deepened

**Constitutional purpose**: Acknowledged that re-audit at
increased evidence may produce SAME tier verdict with
DEEPER architecture. "Same tier, deeper structure" precedent.

#### Q11 v3 — 5-outcome model (Phase 8.14 codification; CURRENT)

Phase 8.13 Bridge audit produced LAW SUB-PATTERN formalization
precedent — KB-Wide LAW REFUSED + LAW SUB-PATTERN promoted.

**5 outcomes**:
- (a) Promote
- (b) Refuse
- (c) Retain
- (d) Refine
- **(e) Law sub-pattern formalization (NEW Phase 8.14)** —
  KB-Wide LAW tier preserved + sub-pattern layer
  introduced/expanded

**Constitutional purpose**: Acknowledged that
constitutionally significant candidates may warrant
LAW-LAYER specialization rather than LAW-TIER promotion.
Hierarchy restructuring as legitimate adjudication outcome.

| version | outcomes | constitutional sophistication |
