Single post template as reading contract

Written by Ji-woon Kim in Design

The single template begins with the post title and only then shows the featured image. That order is different from the static page template, where the image appears before the title.

After the image, TT5 inserts a compact written-by line. The source pattern uses a flex row, a small font size, a narrow 0.2em gap, and a large bottom margin before the body content starts.

The post body remains a constrained prose area. This lab translation keeps the reading column narrow and quiet, because the single route is primarily a complete reading experience.

After the prose, the template adds tags, previous and next navigation, comments, and a more-posts discovery tail. These sections are not decoration; they are part of the route's exit and engagement contract.

The extraction avoids copying TT5 color values. Where TT5 uses accent-6 for borders, the lab maps that role to Axismundi's outline-variant token.
